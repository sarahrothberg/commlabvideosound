COURSE DESCRIPTION

This course explores the fundamentals of sound and video. Students will learn the basics of both audio and video recording using audio field recorders and a variety of cameras as well as editing and exporting in Adobe Premiere. Students are also encouraged to focus on collaboration and contructive critique.
Students will work in teams to produce both a short audio piece and a video short. This 2-credit course meets for the first seven weeks of the semester.
Grading is based on is based on successful completion of all assignments, class journals, participation and attendance.

    • ASSIGNMENT(S)

    Over the next 4 weeks, you will be creating a 3-5 minute video piece in teams of 2-4. Choose ONE of the following options:

  • Option A: A 3 part video series that teaches us "How To..." do something. That "something" is up to you to decide. Let your imagination run wild. But whatever it is, your must teach us how to do it! Each video should be 1-2 mins in length, no shorter, no longer. And the 3 videos can be structured however you like.
  • Option B: A 3-4 minute documentary on a specific street intersection in New York. This documentary can be about the intersection itself or about a character or group of characters that work or live at this intersection.
  • Option C: Re-interpret a short story or poem as a short film.
    • The video pieces can be funny, serious, intense, informative, wacky, or whatever you like. Focus on making solid work in which the style reflects your outcomes.
